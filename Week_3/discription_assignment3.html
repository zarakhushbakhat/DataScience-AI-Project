In this assignment, we perform an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to understand survival factors such as age, gender, class, and fare.

4. Charts and Interpretations

4.1 Age Distribution

📌 Interpretation:
* Most passengers were 20–40 years old.
* Peak at around 24 years (mode).
* Very few infants and elderly passengers.
* Suggests Titanic carried a young to middle-aged majority.

4.2 Fare Distribution

📌 Interpretation:
* Most passengers paid under 50 units.
* Extreme outliers with fares above 500 show wealthy 1st class passengers.
* Median fare is much lower, proving Titanic mostly carried economy travelers.

4.3 Passenger Count by Class

📌 Interpretation:
* Largest group = 3rd Class passengers.
* Fewest passengers = 1st Class, but they had more privileges.
* Highlights strong social and economic diversity.

4.4 Passenger Count by Gender

📌 Interpretation:
* More males than females.
* This imbalance influenced survival analysis, as fewer women had higher survival rates.

4.5 Survival by Gender

📌 Interpretation:
* Women had a much higher survival rate than men.
* Supports the “women and children first” rescue policy.

4.6 Survival by Passenger Class

📌 Interpretation:
* 1st Class passengers survived the most, while 3rd Class passengers had the lowest survival rate.
* Wealth and social class played a critical role in survival chances.

4.7 Age vs Survival (Box Plot)

📌 Interpretation:
* Survivors were often slightly younger.
* Children had better survival chances.
* Elderly passengers had very low survival rates.

4.8 Fare vs Survival (Box Plot)

📌 Interpretation:
* Survivors generally paid higher fares.
* Passengers with cheaper tickets (3rd class) had a lower chance of survival.
* Shows class and wealth strongly influenced survival.

4.9 Correlation Heatmap

📌 Interpretation:
* Fare is positively correlated with survival → higher ticket, higher chance.
* Sex (male) is negatively correlated with survival → males survived less.
* Pclass is negatively correlated with survival → lower-numbered class = better survival.
* Age correlation is weak, but children had slightly better chances.

5. Conclusion

From the Titanic dataset analysis, the main findings are:

  • Gender and Class were the most important factors in survival.
  • Women and children had higher chances.
  • Wealthier (1st Class) passengers survived at much higher rates.
  • The majority of passengers were young adults, but not all survived equally.
  • Fare distribution shows huge inequality, directly linked to survival chances.

This analysis highlights how social and economic inequalities impacted survival during the Titanic disaster.
